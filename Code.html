International Building Codes (IBC) for Thermal Barriers

Depending on a particular application, an Ignition or a Thermal Barrier is required by International Building Code (IBC). Make sure that a Thermal Barrier coating meets the applicable IBC requirements, and an Ignition Barrier coating meets the proper requirements per AC 377, Appendix X.

All Thermal Barrier tests must be performed at full-scale to comply with the requirements of 2006 IBC Section 803.2.1; 2009 IBC Section 803.1.2 and Section 2603.9; 2012 IBC Section 803.1.2 and Section 2603.10 (Special Approval for Thermal Barrier Alternatives). Coatings can also be tested as Ignition Barriers per AC 377, Appendix X.

Thermal Barriers

What is a Thermal Barrier?
With regard to spray foam insulation, a Thermal Barrier is a material that separates SPF from all interior spaces of a structure and has successfully completed a 15-minute fire test per established criteria

Prescriptive Thermal Barrier
The standard for Thermal Barriers, called a Prescriptive Thermal Barrier, recognized by International Building Code (IBC) for use in walls and ceilings is 1/2” gypsum board (aka dry wall or sheet rock). Plywood with a nominal thickness of 3/4€” is not prescriptive per se, but is acceptable for use in flooring.

Equivalent (Non-Prescriptive) Thermal Barriers
Other materials must submit to and successfully complete full-scale fire testing in order to be approved as Equivalent Thermal Barriers. IBC 2603.4 mandates two full-scale 15-minute tests: one under the criteria of ASTM E119 or UL 263; the other under the criteria of NFPA 286, UL 1715, UL 1040, or FM 4880

Special Approval
Deemed an Alternate Assembly, Spray Foam Insulation that is covered with a protective coating, or left exposed altogether, is subject to separate requirements and does not need to comply with IBC 2603.4. Rather, large scale fire testing criteria for SPF is specified under NFPA 286, UL 1715, UL 1040, and FM 4880. Additionally, a protective coating must be tested on a given brand of foam in order to be applied as a Thermal Barrier over that foam.

Ignition Barriers

Ignition Barrier Coatings for Spray Foam Insulation

What is an Ignition Barrier?
In certain situations, requirements for Thermal Barriers are reduced to allow the installation of an Ignition Barrier. Specifically, IBC 2603.4.1.6 permits Ignition Barriers to be installed in restricted-access areas like crawls spaces and unvented attics.

Prescriptive Ignition Barriers
Prescriptive Ignition Barriers include 3/8” gypsum board, 1/4” hardboard, 1.5 mineral fiber insulation, 3/8” particleboard, 0.016 steel, and 1/4” wood structural panels.

Special Approval
As with Thermal Barriers, there are Alternative Ignition Barrier Assemblies. Specifically for SPF, AC-377 Appendix X outlines criteria for testing Ignition Barriers via a modified NPFA 286 test.
