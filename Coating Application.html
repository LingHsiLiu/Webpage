COATING APPLICATION

Characteristics to Consider

Thermal barrier coatings for spray foam insulation can be used in residential applications such as over attic insulation or crawl space insulation, but can also be applied to commercial building insulation and used for military applications. When these thermal barrier coatings are applied, there are a few characteristics to consider:

Spread Rate
How much square footage will a gallon of a coating cover? The greater the area, the more profitable an application will be.

Substrate Compatibility
How many materials will a coating adhere to? With more compatible materials, fewer primers and prep materials are needed.

Ease of Application
Can you apply the coating quickly with a sprayer? Is time-consuming manual rolling and brushing needed?

Viscosity
Is it thick or thin? Thinner coatings are easier to apply, but thicker coatings need fewer coats to reach a required thickness.

Mixing
Do you need to mix multiple components, or can you simply stir a coating prior to an application?

Clean-Up Process
How much time will you have to dedicate to cleaning up?

Shelf Life
How long will a coating last in your inventory?

Listings
Is the coating listed by companies like WHI (Warnock Hersey), Underwriters Laboratories (UL), or CSA (CSA Group)?
