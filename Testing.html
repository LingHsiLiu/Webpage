Testing

The International Building Code (IBC) mandates that Spray Polyurethane Foam be separated from the interior of the building by a 15-minute Thermal Barrier, or other approved covering. IBC requires certified testing to ensure codec compliance. Any company that claims to be compliant should supply you proof.

All Thermal Barrier coatings must pass a full-scale test on each of the manufacturer types of open-cell and close-cell foams. Specific spray foam products from manufacturers like Bayer MaterialScience, Lapolla, Gaco Western, Quadrant, Icynene, Premium Spray Products, NCFI, SWD, etc., must be individually tested to be used as a code-compliant Thermal Barrier coating.

If a coating has not passed a full-scale test on a manufacturers foam, it CANNOT be used on that foam. There are no exceptions in the IBC Code!

ROOM CORNER BURN TESTS

Test Setting

  • 8’ x 12’ room
  • 8’ wood-framed walls (24”€ on-center)
  • 30”- wide doorway
  • 5/8” gypsum board exterior sheathing

With regard to materials, the thickness of both the coating and the foam are specified by its manufacturer prior to testing. Typically, the materials are tested at maximum application thickness.

The room assembly is split into four quadrants and a temperature sensor is placed in each quadrant. Three additional temperature sensors are placed at the heat source, at the center of the ceiling, and at the doorway. An optical sensor is used to measure the amount of smoke produced by the test fire. Data is collected every six seconds throughout the duration of the test.

Measurements:

  • The extent of fire growth
  • The rate of heat released
  • The amount of smoke released
  • The occurrence of flashover (measured via pieces of paper)
  • The incidence of flames exiting the doorway

The flame source is a propane-fueled burner that is positioned in one of the room back corners, opposite the door. The burner is elevated one foot above the floor and separated one inch from the walls.

NFPA 286

NFPA 286 standards outline a 15-minute phase that begins when the propane burner is ignited and set to output 40kW of energy (roughly 600Âº F at the source). For 5 minutes, the burner is maintained at 40kW and subsequently turned up to 160kW (roughly 1200Âº F at the source), where it is maintained for 10 minutes before the gas is turned off and the test is over.

AC-377 APPENDIX X (MODIFIED NFPA 286)

AC377 Appendix X allows for a modified version of NFPA 286 testing standards to indicate whether or not a coating meets the criteria of an Ignition Barrier. Like with the standard NFPA, the modified version of the test begins when the burner is turned on and maintained at a 40kW output. However, the output is maintained for only 4 minutes and 18 seconds before the gas is turned off and the test is over.
